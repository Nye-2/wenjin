# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Wenjin serves academic research and writing users who need help turning vague research intent into traceable work: undergraduate students, ordinary master's students, and research-adjacent users who benefit from structured guidance, plus administrators who maintain models, capabilities, pricing, and runtime settings.

## Product Purpose

Wenjin is an AI workbench for academic research and writing. It keeps chat, expert execution, evidence, documents, decisions, sandbox work, review gates, and run history inside one workspace so users can move from question framing to literature work, experiments, writing, revision, and accepted outputs without losing provenance.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, quiet, exacting. The interface should feel like a serious research workbench: compact, readable, calm under long sessions, and confident without becoming decorative.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic chatbot, a marketing SaaS dashboard, a raw developer log viewer, or an ornamental academic theme. Avoid decorative ancient-style motifs, fixed technical sidebars in the default UX, gratuitous gradients, decorative orbs, and over-carded layouts.

## Design Principles

- Keep the research workflow legible: show what is happening, what needs review, and what was accepted.
- Preserve evidence and authorship boundaries: high-risk claims, citations, and document edits must feel reviewable before commit.
- Favor dense but calm product UI: trusted chrome, restrained color, compact panels, and stable controls.
- Make agent work observable without exposing raw logs by default.
- Use existing `--wjn-*` design tokens and converge old compatibility surfaces instead of adding parallel visual systems.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use readable contrast, stable layouts, keyboard-accessible controls, semantic labels, and reduced-motion-safe feedback. Long-running AI work should provide visible state changes so users are not left staring at an empty surface.
